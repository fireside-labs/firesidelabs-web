Pruning is Free at 5%

A Surprising Finding on Parameter Efficiency

Conventional wisdom: remove X% of parameters, lose Y% of performance. Trade-offs are always real.

We found an exception. On Gemma-4-31B, removing 5% of parameters via magnitude pruning produces no measurable performance loss on HumanEval, MMLU, and general knowledge benchmarks. The model runs faster and uses less VRAM. Accuracy stays identical.

5% smaller. 4% faster. Same accuracy. This is a genuine efficiency win with no cost.

Results

Variant HumanEval MMLU Latency Memory
Base 95.1% 85.6% 342ms ~22GB
Prune-5% 95.1% 85.6% 328ms (4%↓) ~20.8GB (6%↓)
Prune-7% 93.3% (-1.8pt) 86.2% 315ms (7.9%↓) ~20.5GB
Prune-10% 89.6% (-5.5pt) 81.2% 298ms (12.9%↓) ~18.5GB

Key observation: At 5% pruning, zero loss. At 7%, degradation begins (1.8pt). At 10%, significant cost (5.5pt).

Critical Caveat

These scores have NOT yet been validated under letter-shuffle contamination probing (see Paper 2). Before publishing the "free" claim: run letter-shuffle on Prune-5% and Prune-7%. If contamination sensitivity increases, the result is questionable. If sensitivity matches base model, the claim is robust.

Why This Happens

Either Gemma-4-31B is over-parameterized for these benchmarks, or the smallest 5% of parameters contribute almost nothing to output. Most likely: Q4_K_M quantization already rounds very small weights to zero, so magnitude pruning at 5% removes parameters that are already functionally zero.

Practical Implications

When This Matters

Limited VRAM: Use Prune-5% to free 1.2GB of VRAM with zero accuracy cost. Latency-critical: 4% faster with same output quality. Cost-optimized inference: Same model capability at lower compute cost.

When It Doesn't

Abundant VRAM: Pruning adds complexity without benefit. Max-accuracy requirements: Don't take the risk. Models being fine-tuned: Pruning before fine-tuning may be suboptimal.

Next Steps

This finding is robust within Gemma-4-31B and Q4_K_M quantization. Replication needed on:

• Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen3-14B, Mistral-Nemo-12B (architectural generalization)

• Different quantizations (Q6_K, IQ3, unquantized)

• Fine-tuning after pruning (recovery of accuracy)

At 5% pruning, Gemma-4-31B shows no measurable accuracy loss on code, knowledge, and reasoning benchmarks. This is either genuine over-parameterization or quantization artifact. Either way, pragmatically valuable.
